## 1. Purpose

Produce a 13-asset static-graphic marketing kit (+ multiple copy variations per asset) for the "BUSINESS UNLOCKED" summit (May 9, 2026, PTTC Pasay). Kit gets distributed to 6 partner channels who run their own ads / organic posting. Variations exist so each channel has fresh creative and so we can A/B test hooks.

Design + copy philosophy follows **Alex Hormozi + Sabri Suby** principles:
- Value equation: dream outcome × perceived likelihood ÷ time × effort
- Godfather offers (too-good-to-refuse framing)
- Specific numbers, not adjectives
- Pattern-interrupt visual hierarchy
- Cold-traffic problem-agitate-solve hooks
- Earned scarcity (real seat count / real early-bird deadline)

Copy must be grounded in **current PH SME pain** (fuel costs, peso, political uncertainty, consumer pullback) — no generic "in these challenging times" filler. See Section 5 for research hooks.

## 5.5 Shared Hormozi + Suby Ad Rulebook

Both Hormozi and Suby converge on the same 12 principles. These are the rules every asset must obey:

1. **Avatar call-out first** — open with WHO, not the product. ("Attention Filipino business owners doing ₱100K–₱5M/month…")
2. **Godfather/Grand Slam offer** — stack value, show total, reveal price last. Make the ratio absurd.
3. **Specific numbers beat adjectives** — "₱172.90/L diesel" > "rising costs"; "2,000 seats, 67 taken" > "limited seats".
4. **Problem → Agitate → Solve, with twist the knife** — name the pain, compound the cost of inaction, THEN introduce the mechanism.
5. **Yes-ladder in long captions** — 3–5 questions the reader answers "yes" to before the pitch.
6. **Yellow/gold highlighter on ONE power word per graphic** — never the whole headline.
7. **Big ugly headlines beat pretty ones** — direct-response energy, not luxury brand. 60–80% of canvas.
8. **Risk-reversal badge on every commercial asset** — "Walang sulit, refund agad" corner badge.
9. **Social proof strip** — logos or "Hosted by ExpoU + Gencys Group" bar along the bottom.
10. **Real, earned scarcity only** — use seat count + dated deadline, honor it. No fake timers.
11. **Before/After transformation frame** — literal two-column "without the system / with the system" visuals work for SMEs because they're concrete.
12. **Direct CTA with exact next step** — "Tap link → Pay ₱1,999 → Get instant e-ticket + pre-summit playbook". Never "Learn More."

**Taglish softening rule:** keep the structure English, soften with Tagalog connectors ("Para sa mga owners na…", "Kung tapos ka na sa…", "Pagod ka na ba…"). Never bro-guru tone. Write like a sharp friend who ran the numbers.
